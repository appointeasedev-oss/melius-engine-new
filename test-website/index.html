Who was Marcus Aurelius?

Marcus Aurelius Antoninus (121–180 CE) was Roman emperor from 161 to 180 CE and is often remembered as history’s clearest example of a philosopher king. While leading campaigns and governing a vast empire, he wrote private notes on character, mortality, and responsibility.

Reflection from Stoicism

“You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength.”

Why “Philosopher King” fits

  • Duty over comfort: He treated power as service, not privilege.
  • Reason over impulse: He practiced self-command in public life.
  • Virtue over fame: His writings focus on integrity, not applause.
